Hi, I'm John Niedermeyer, a designer based in New York.

My work has spanned startups, newsrooms, and scaled platforms, shaping products and brands at the intersection of technology, media, and culture. Most recently, I was Principal Designer at Resy, working across the product suite from Consumer to Restaurant, helping shape experiences across the ecosystem. Before that, as Head of Design, I led Product, Brand, Experiential, and Editorial design through the company's post-Series A growth.

Originally from Buffalo, N.Y., I started my career in Boston and have spent most of the past two decades living and working in New York City. Earlier on, I spent nearly eight years at The New York Times, with roles spanning the Newsroom, Opinion, Elections, and Product.

Today, I'm focused on helping thoughtful teams build products with clarity, ambition, and craft. I'm especially drawn to work that connects business goals, user needs, and strong editorial or brand sensibilities.

Colophon

This site is built more like a small publication than a product shell: hand-tuned, lightweight, and close to the material.

Type

The site is set in Söhne, mostly in light, book, and halbfett cuts, with italics reserved for contrast, emphasis, and a little mischief.

Build

It runs as a deliberately simple static site: semantic HTML, bespoke CSS, and just enough JavaScript to handle theme state, navigation behavior, and a few quality-of-life details.

Preview

Local previews come up through the repo Make targets, which wrap a tiny Python server so the work can stay inspectable in a browser without adding framework overhead.

Deploy

Shipping goes through a staged shell deploy that syncs the managed site files and refreshes cache busted asset URLs on the way out, keeping the source tree clean.
