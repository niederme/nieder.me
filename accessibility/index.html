Accessibility statement

Target

WCAG 2.2 AA, as an ongoing standard to work toward

Scope

The portfolio, work index, case studies, and supporting site pages on nieder.me

Updated

April 4, 2026

Commitment

I want this site to be usable by as many people as possible. That includes people using screen readers, keyboard navigation, zoom, reduced-motion settings, and other assistive technologies or browser accommodations.

My goal is to maintain the site in alignment with WCAG 2.2 Level AA over time, while continuing to improve older content and spot regressions as the portfolio evolves.

Current approach

The site is built with semantic HTML, explicit headings, labeled navigation, keyboard-reachable controls, and responsive layouts that should hold up under browser zoom and narrow viewports. Shared components such as theme toggles, carousels, and navigation controls are also wired with ARIA state and labeling where appropriate.

I also try to preserve readable contrast, maintain visible interaction states, and respect reduced-motion preferences where motion-heavy media behavior could become distracting.

Known limitations

Some older or image-heavy portfolio materials may still need richer long-form text alternatives. A few media assets and demos may also be lighter on captions, transcripts, or equivalent descriptive context than I want them to be.

If you run into a problem, I would much rather hear about it than leave it unresolved.

Feedback

If something on this site is difficult to access or use, please email john@nieder.me.

If you can, include the page URL, the assistive technology or browser you were using, and a short note about what went wrong. That gives me the best chance of reproducing and fixing the issue quickly.
