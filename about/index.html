Overview

Hi, I'm John Niedermeyer, a designer based in New York. My work has spanned startups, newsrooms, and scaled platforms, shaping products and brands at the intersection of technology, media, and culture.

Most recently, I was Principal Designer at Resy, working across the product suite from Consumer to Restaurant and helping shape experiences across the ecosystem. Before that, as Head of Design, I led Product, Brand, Experiential, and Editorial design through the company's post-Series A growth.

Background

Originally from Buffalo, N.Y., I started my career in Boston and have spent most of the past two decades living and working in New York City. Earlier on, I spent nearly eight years at The New York Times, with roles spanning the Newsroom, Opinion, Elections, and Product.

My work is usually strongest where editorial judgment, product clarity, and visual craft all need to coexist. I’m drawn to teams that want both strong taste and strong systems, and to products that need to feel considered all the way from first impression through shipped detail.

Today

Today, I'm focused on helping thoughtful teams build products with clarity, ambition, and craft. I'm especially drawn to work that connects business goals, user needs, and strong editorial or brand sensibilities.
